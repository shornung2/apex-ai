The Challenge

Your best people are buried
in work that shouldn't need them.

The average B2B sales or presales professional spends less than a third of their time actually selling. The rest? Research, proposals, presentation prep, content creation, and follow-up. Important work — but not work that requires your best human talent to do it manually.

01
Hours Lost to Manual Research
Before every meeting, your reps piece together account intel from LinkedIn, news searches, and CRM notes. It takes hours and still leaves gaps. When they walk in underprepared, opportunities slip.
02
Proposals That Take Days, Not Hours
Every custom proposal pulls someone off a live opportunity to write. Quality varies by author. Your best proposals are exceptional — but they're inconsistent, and speed is often sacrificed for quality.
03
Institutional Knowledge That Goes Unused
Your competitive intelligence, win/loss insights, product knowledge, and battle cards exist somewhere — in documents, email threads, someone's head. Getting it into the hands of the right rep at the right moment never happens consistently.
04
Marketing Output Bottlenecked by Capacity
Thought leadership, campaign content, follow-up assets, and sales enablement materials constantly compete for a small team's bandwidth. The content strategy is clear; the time to execute it never is.
